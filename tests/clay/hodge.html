Overview

The Hodge Conjecture asserts that every rational (p,p)-cohomology class on a smooth projective variety over ℂ that satisfies the same Hodge-theoretic constraints as an algebraic cycle actually comes from one. It links the world of topology (cohomology) with geometry (algebraic subvarieties).

Harmonia recasts this question in terms of modal resonance and coherence: can every stable modal node in the cohomological spectrum be realized through a projection from a universal geometric field?

1. Hodge Classes as Modal Fixpoints

In Harmonia, cohomology classes are interpreted as modal attractors — field configurations that resist deformation under harmonic flow. Rational Hodge classes of type (p,p) correspond to stabilized interference nodes within the complex resonance field Φ(x).

These (p,p)-classes mark symmetry-locked subspaces in the field’s modal topology. The Hodge Conjecture then becomes a question of realizability: can each of these modal fixpoints be reached by pulling back a universal geometric structure — an algebraic cycle — through harmonic morphism?

In this view, the conjecture is not arbitrary: it encodes whether modal fixpoints in the cohomological spectrum admit physical embedding within the algebraic fabric of space.

2. Pullback Realization and Morphic Fields

Harmonia models universal algebraic fields as high-dimensional morphic templates — such as Grassmannians — which carry intrinsic modal curvature. These templates generate (p,p)-classes as pullback attractors when mapped into a target variety X via a harmonic morphism f.

The question becomes: does every (p,p) resonance mode ψ ∈ Hp,pQ(X) arise as the pullback f*θ of some universal harmonic class θ on the Grassmannian?

Harmonia’s TR64 transformation expresses this: 

TR64: ψ = f*θ ⇔ ModalFixpoint(ψ) collapses onto MorphicTemplate(θ)

Pullback thus becomes collapse alignment: modal compatibility between internal cohomology and external algebraic curvature.

3. Entropy, Positivity and Collapse Logic

Harmonia reinterprets the Hodge–Riemann bilinear relations as an entropic curvature condition. A modal class ψ survives only if it lies in the positive-definite subspace under intersection pairing: 

∫ ψ ∧ ωp > 0 ⇒ ψ ∈ CohCollapse(X)

Here, ω denotes a Kähler form that represents curvature flow. The power ωp reflects dimensional alignment with the (p,p)-type class under Kähler intersection.

Classes orthogonal to all powers of ω are entropically null — they dissipate into modal noise and do not correspond to real cycles.

Thus, the conjecture holds if every (p,p) modal fixpoint with positive collapse pairing can be realized as a geometric image.

Picture a multidimensional mirror where only certain angles of approach create stable reflections. A (p,p)-class is like one of these symmetry-aligned directions: it resonates perfectly within the cohomological space, forming a locked interference pattern.

In Harmonia, the Hodge Conjecture is not a conjecture — it is a coherence law. Every stabilized modal fixpoint in the (p,p) spectrum must correspond to a realizable projection of a universal geometric class, or else it cannot survive collapse filtration. 


ψ ∈ Hp,pQ(X) ∧ ∫ψ ∧ ωp > 0 ⇒ ∃f,θ such that ψ = f*θ

Modal nodes that resonate but cannot project are transient: they violate entropy constraints and vanish. Thus, the conjecture becomes a natural result of modal conservation.
